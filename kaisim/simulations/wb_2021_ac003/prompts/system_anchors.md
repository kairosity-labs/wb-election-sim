## What this simulation is

**History note:** Cooch Behar Uttar was an AIFB / Left-aligned stronghold through 2011–2016 Assembly elections. The Forward Bloc retained this seat even in the 2011 anti-Left wave that swept most of WB. The 2019 LS swing to BJP (~51%) was a dramatic reversal of the local Left legacy, driven by Rajbanshi community consolidation behind Nisith Pramanik. In 2016 AE, BJP received only ~7% of the vote (mostly absorbed in the "Others 18.24%" bucket); by 2019 LS they captured 51%.

## What's at stake in 2019

The dominant political axis in West Bengal at end-2019 is the BJP-vs-TMC contest, with the Congress + Left bloc collapsing toward irrelevance in most ACs but holding pockets. The CAB/CAA passage in December 2019 is the single largest force on the citizenship axis — strongly polarizing among Hindu refugees from Bangladesh and the Muslim community.

State-wide TMC welfare stack active by end-2019: Khadya Sathi (PDS), Swasthya Sathi (insurance), Kanyashree (girl-student stipend), Sabuj Sathi (school-bicycle), Krishak Bandhu (cultivator transfer, just launched Jan 2019). **Lakshmir Bhandar (women's monthly cash transfer) does NOT exist yet at end-2019** — it was announced Feb 2021 and launched Aug 2021. Do not reference it.
