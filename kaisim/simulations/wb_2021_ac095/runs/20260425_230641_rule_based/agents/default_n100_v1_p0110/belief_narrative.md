## [2023-12-31] (reflection, importance 8)
I thought when Delhi finally said our name, when Shantanu came with papers and promises, it meant we would finally have dignity—that Harichand Thakur's dream was coming true at last. But then I watched our own people tear the Thakurbari apart, fighting like animals over power, and I realized that no party in Delhi, no politician's name, can give us what we have to build ourselves. That hurt more than my back ever did—the understanding that I had waited fifty years for something that could never come from outside.

## [2024-02-29] (reaction, importance 4)
Mamatabala in Rajya Sabha—yes, I heard. TMC trying to show they also have Matua blood, Matua voice. But what does it mean? Another chair in Delhi for another person to sit in and make promises. I voted for Shantanu thinking Delhi had finally seen us. Now I see Delhi sees us only when there is an election coming, when we are useful. Mamatabala or Shantaru—they are both far from Akaipur, both far from my mother's prayers at Thakurbari.

## [2024-03-31] (reaction, importance 2)
Electoral bonds, corruption lists—these are things for people who read newspapers in cities. I do not read English news. My son tells me sometimes what he hears in Kerala. What does it matter to me in Akaipur whether BJP took more money or less money? They promised us papers. The papers have not come. That is what I know.

## [2024-05-20] (reaction, importance 6)
Fourteen certificates. My son called from Kerala and told me—fourteen pieces of paper for how many lakhs of us? I asked my wife: 'Is this the promise, or is this the insult dressed as a promise?' She did not answer. I have waited fifty years. Fourteen certificates five days before an election—this is not dignity, this is a show. I will vote again, but now I vote knowing that Delhi's promises and Delhi's papers are not the same thing.
