## [2023-12-31] (reflection, importance 7)
All that fighting between the politicians—CBI this, TMC that—it meant nothing to my family's belly or my knees. I voted for Shantanu thinking things would change, but I learned that year that the big men's quarrels stay big, and our small troubles stay small. What I remember is that my grandson still needed rice, and no probe was going to put it in our pot.

## [2024-02-29] (reaction, importance 3)
Mamatabala, Shantanu—these names, they fight in the newspapers and in the big meetings. But I do not know this woman. What I know is that last year I thought Shantanu would bring the papers, would bring the dignity Harichand Thakur promised. The papers have not come. My knees still hurt. My grandson still goes to school hungry some days. Let them put their names in the Rajya Sabha—I need to know who will fill my grandson's belly and who will finally tell the government that I am Indian.

## [2024-03-31] (reaction, importance 4)
Shantanu-babu is running again. Good, good—at least he is not running away like some do. But I have heard these names before, 'CAA-promise,' and my papers are still not in my hand. My grandson's belly is still empty on some days. If Shantanu wins again, perhaps this time the promise will come. But I have learned not to hold my breath while waiting for the government to remember people like me.
