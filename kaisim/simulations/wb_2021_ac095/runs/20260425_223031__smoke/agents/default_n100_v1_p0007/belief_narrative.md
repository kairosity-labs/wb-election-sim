## [2020-06-30] (reflection, importance 9)
Those months taught me that voting against something out of anger is not the same as voting for something out of hope. I watched my students' fathers return from the cities with nothing, watched the trucks pass our gates, and I realized that swapping TMC's corrupt indifference for the Center's absent indifference had solved nothing — I had simply distributed the same failure across two governments instead of fixing it. That was when I understood that my 2019 vote, cast in anger against decay, had not purchased the competence I thought I was buying.
