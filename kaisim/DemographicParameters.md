# Demographic Parameters of the West Bengal Voter Population: A Structured Reference for Causal Modeling of the 2026 Assembly Election

**Compilation date:** 24 April 2026 (Phase 1 polled 23 April; Phase 2 polls 29 April; counting 4 May 2026)
**Total 2026 electorate after SIR + supplementary additions:** ~6.82 crore (68.25 million), across 294 Assembly constituencies, of which 68 are SC-reserved and 16 ST-reserved ([Wikipedia – 2026 WBLA election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_West_Bengal_Legislative_Assembly_election); [Zee News – reserved seats](https://zeenews.india.com/india/the-power-of-84-why-reserved-seats-are-the-ultimate-kingmakers-in-bengal-3039842.html)).

This document catalogs demographic parameters that can function as nodes in a structural causal model (SCM) of the 2026 West Bengal election. For each parameter it provides: (a) definition, (b) current distribution with concrete numbers, (c) historical voting correlation from CSDS-Lokniti post-poll surveys (2019, 2021, 2024), and (d) 2026 salience. A prioritized list of the top 15 SCM nodes appears at the end.

### SCM-relevant observations on causal interactions:
- **SIR × Religion × Geography**: SIR deletion impact is geographically and religiously concentrated — Muslim-majority border districts (Murshidabad, Malda, UD) and Matua-dense districts (N24Pgs, Nadia) show highest absolute deletion counts, creating competing narratives.
- **Welfare × Gender × Class**: Lakshmir Bhandar is the strongest single correlated variable with TMC vote among women, especially poor/lower-class rural women; this bloc is least affected by urban discourse shifts like RG Kar.
- **CAA × Caste × Citizenship anxiety**: The same CAA that potentially benefits Matuas-Namasudras also creates NRC fear among Muslims; the net effect is community-specific and has produced counter-intuitive outcomes (CAA implementation in March 2024 did NOT produce the Hindu consolidation BJP expected in 2024 LS).
- **Caste × Region**: Upper-caste Bhadralok Hindutva is concentrated in urban Kolkata, while OBC-Hindutva is concentrated in Jangalmahal + industrial belt, indicating separate Hindu mobilization pathways.
- **Violence × Turnout**: 2026 Phase 1 recorded ~92% turnout (highest ever), with highest rises in areas BJP lost narrowly in 2021; the central-force deployment has potentially moderated the TMC's historical booth-capture advantage.
- **Migrant status × SIR**: Out-migrant workers are over-represented in "absent/shifted" deletion category, damaging TMC in rural labour-export districts but generating a counter-narrative of "Mamata defending Bengali mazdoor."

### Data caveats for SCM construction:
- Census 2011 caste/religion data is 15 years old; Muslim share has likely risen further given 2001–11 growth trend of 21.8% vs Hindu 10.8%.
- No caste census beyond SC/ST; OBC sub-caste shares are academic estimates, not official.
- CSDS-Lokniti WB 2024 disaggregates are limited compared to the richer 2021 post-poll dataset; some 2024 findings are based on national-sample Bengal subsets.
- SIR deletion religion/gender breakdowns from booth-level analyses ([Free Press Journal](https://www.freepressjournal.in/analysis/west-bengal-sir-row-myths-data-and-the-voter-deletions-that-could-reshape-2026-elections); [Outlook](https://www.outlookindia.com/elections/how-does-sir-impact-bengals-muslim-majority-constituencies)) are estimates derived from pre/post-SIR roll comparison, not official ECI-released breakdowns — treat as directional.
- Female LFPR figures vary sharply by survey: PLFS 2018-19 gave 17.5%, PLFS 2022-23 gives 33.8% — the sharp rise reflects revised methodology and self-employed-unpaid-helper counting, not necessarily real change.
- Matua voter count varies: Mahasangha claims ~1.8 crore; academic (Sinharay, Bandyopadhyay) estimates are lower. Use ranges in SCM.
- The 2024 CSDS-Lokniti post-poll for WB was a small subsample; the cleanest CSDS data point is the 2021 post-poll ([Lokniti report 2021](https://lokniti.org/media/PDF-upload/1622695848_3736100_download_report.pdf)).
