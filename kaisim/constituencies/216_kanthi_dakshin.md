# AC 216 — Kanthi Dakshin (GEN)

**District:** Purba Medinipur · **Region:** Purba Medinipur Coast · **LS:** Kanthi (PC 31) · **2026 Phase:** TBD · **Total electors:** per CEO WB SIR 2026 row

> Pilot #8 — tests the **Mahishya OBC + Adhikari family stronghold + Kanthi coast** axis. One of the most Mahishya-dense ACs in WB.

## 1. Demographic snapshot

- Composition: **Contai-I (Kanthi-I) CDB + Contai municipality (Kanthi town)** + parts of **Deshapran (Kanthi-II) CDB** (A, Delimitation 2008)
- Kanthi town = political nerve-centre of **Adhikari family** (Sisir, Suvendu, Dibyendu, Soumendu)
- Digha–Shankarpur–Junput coast (fishing villages) spillover into AC; ~50 km from Haldia petro-port

## 2. Caste composition

District baseline: Hindu 85.24, Muslim 14.59, SC 14.63, ST 0.55 (A, Census 2011). Kanthi sub-division more Hindu-skewed than district average.

## 4. Economic profile

- **Coastal fisheries:** Hilsa/prawn/pomfret landing; Kaibarta boat crews
- **Haldia-adjacent industrial spillover:** commuter labour, contractor networks
- **Agriculture:** paddy (kharif), betel leaf (**Purba Medinipur = India's largest paan-exporting district**), cashew, vegetables
- **MSME cluster in Kanthi town:** rice mills, cashew processing, ice factories, boat repair

Livelihood pressures moderate — neither Sundarbans-precarious nor Salt-Lake affluent.

## 5. Education

- AC literacy ~89–91% (E); Kanthi town urban pockets cross 93–94% (E)
- Purba Medinipur is **WB's most literate district** (88.60%, A)
- Contai College, P.K. College, Mahishya Samaj educational trusts; high civic engagement

## 6. Welfare footprint

Moderate scheme dependence vs northern/Jangalmahal ACs. Lakshmir Bhandar has substantial uptake (women ~49% of roll). Krishak Bandhu high among Mahishya paddy/paan cultivators. Swasthya Sathi near-universal. **PM-Kisan** significant reach — BJP campaigns aggressively. Welfare here is a credit-attribution contest (TMC LB vs BJP PM-Kisan/Ujjwala/Awas), not a dependence vote.

## 7. Political dynamics

**The Adhikari arc:** Sisir Adhikari built the network (Cong→AITC 1998, Kanthi LS 2009/2014/2019); Suvendu engineered 2007 Nandigram movement; brothers split 2020–24, all four now BJP as of 2024.

| Year | Contest | Winner | Party | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2011 AE | AC 216 | Dibyendu Adhikari | AITC | Mamata sweep |
| 2016 AE | AC 216 | Dibyendu Adhikari | AITC | Expanded margin |
| 2019 LS | Kanthi | Sisir Adhikari | AITC | BJP climb in district |
| **2021 AE** | AC 216 | **Sumita Singha** | **AITC** | Suvendu contested Nandigram instead; AITC held AC despite family split |
| **2024 LS** | Kanthi | **Soumendu Adhikari** | **BJP** | Full family→BJP consolidation; ~48k margin; AC segment BJP-led |

**2026 outlook:**
- **TMC:** Sumita Singha (incumbent) re-nomination near-certain
- **BJP:** Adhikari-aligned Mahishya face (Soumendu-proxy)
- **Swing driver:** Mahishya bloc direction (50% of electorate) + Adhikari-family-unified BJP uplift (+2 to +4 pp) vs Lakshmir Bhandar women-voter floor
- **Base rate:** TMC 46–51, BJP 43–48 — marginal / BJP-lean; margin under 5 pp either way

**Hindu consolidation ceiling:** Muslim share ~5% → BJP polarisation toolkit has limited headroom; contest is **intra-Hindu Mahishya arithmetic**.
