# AC 014 — Madarihat (ST)

**District:** Alipurduar · **Region:** North Bengal Plains · **LS:** Alipurduar · **2026 Phase:** TBD · **Total electors:** 199,912 (98,918 M / 100,990 F / 4 TG / 226 PS)

> Pilot #10 — tests the **Dooars tea-garden workers + Oraon/Munda ST adivasi + ₹300/day wage politics** axis. The 2024 Madarihat by-election flipped this seat from BJP→TMC for the first time.

## 1. Demographic snapshot

- Composition: **Madarihat-Birpara CDB** (tea-garden belt); notable gardens Birpara, Dalgaon, Dalmore, Dheklapara, Lankapara, Tulsipara, Ramjhora, Hantapara (A, Delimitation 2008)
- Birpara town = main semi-urban node (~12–15% urban in AC, below district 20.61%)
- Jaldapara National Park borders the AC; elephant-human conflict live issue

## 5. Economic profile

- **Tea garden dependence:** ~60–70% of households directly on garden wages + allied (factory, transport, forest produce) (E)
- **Tea gardens in AC:** ~25–35 of state's ~408 gardens (D)
- **Wage:** ₹250/day (2022–25 tripartite); unions demand ₹350–₹450 on minimum-wage notification grounds. **TMC 2026 manifesto: ₹300/day** (D, [ThePrint](https://theprint.in/politics/how-tea-gardens-in-north-bengal-key-to-poll-fortunes-of-bjp-tmc-have-kept-both-guessing/641016/))
- **In-kind benefits** (ration, garden housing, firewood, medical) ₹80–₹120/day; **closed/abandoned gardens** (Dheklapara, Ramjhora, Dalmore historical) strip these (D)
- Seasonal plucking Mar–Nov; lean Dec–Feb drives migration to Kerala (construction), TN (Tirupur textiles), Maharashtra, Bhutan day labour (C/D)
- **BPL modelled ~45–55%** of households (E)

## 6. Education

- AC literacy ~68–72% (E) — significantly below district 78.57%; garden-line schooling gap
- Female literacy ~60–65% (E)
- ST female literacy ~55% (E) — Oraon girls drop by Class 8 for plucking
- Birpara College (UG) main access; Alipurduar College, NBU (Siliguri) onward progression limited

## 7. Welfare footprint

- **Cha Sundari** (TMC, 2020) — pucca houses for landless tea workers on garden land; target 3.7L HH state-wide; Madarihat one of highest-uptake ACs; ~15k–25k beneficiaries modelled (D)
- **Lakshmir Bhandar** ~70k–80k women (SC/ST rate ₹1,200) (E)
- **Swasthya Sathi** 85–92% coverage via camp mode (D/E)
- **Kanyashree / Rupashree** high enrolment
- **PMAY-G** overlaps Cha Sundari — BJP credit via MoS John Barla legacy
- **FRA (Forest Rights Act)** titles slow; live grievance

**Key signal:** Cha Sundari + Lakshmir Bhandar + ₹300 wage pitch traction.

**2026 outlook:**
- **AITC:** Jayprakash Toppo (incumbent, Oraon-Christian by-election winner) (D)
- **BJP:** likely ST Christian/Oraon counter — Rahul Lohar / Dhiraj Tigga / fresh Munda face
- **Base rate:** TMC 46–49%, BJP 40–44%, Left/INC/CPRM 4–6%, others 3–5%

### Tea-worker union cross-pressures

- **Bharatiya Tea Workers Union (BMS/RSS)** — active in 199 of 408 state gardens; BJP booth infrastructure (D)
- **INTTUC (TMC)** — expanded post-2021; ₹300 wage + Cha Sundari pitch
- **CITU (CPI-M)** — legacy, shrinking
- **PTWU (John Barla)** — weakened post-Barla-marginalisation
