# AC 007 — Dinhata (GEN)

**District:** Cooch Behar · **Region:** North Bengal Plains · **LS:** Cooch Behar (SC) · **2026 Phase:** Phase 1 (23 April 2026) · **Total electors:** 292,378 (150,678 M / 141,700 F / 0 TG / 318 PS)

> Pilot #4 — tests the **Rajbanshi identity + Kamtapur autonomy + Muslim-border pocket + Nisith Pramanik political arc** axis. Most volatile AE seat of 2021 cycle (57-vote BJP win; 164,089-vote TMC by-poll flip 6 months later).

## 1. Demographic snapshot

- Composition: **Dinhata municipality + entire Dinhata-II CDB + 4 GPs of Dinhata-I CDB** (Bhetaguri-I, Dinhata Gram-I, Dinhata Gram-II, Putimari-I) (A, Delimitation Commission Order 18)
- AC is GEN within SC-reserved Cooch Behar LS — **structural tension**: Rajbanshi SC vote is decisive but no Rajbanshi is bound to the ticket
- **SIR deletions** — Cooch Behar district total ~2.42L; AC 7 share ~36k estimated pro-rata (E, not published per-AC)

## 2. Religion (AC-specific, CDB rollup)

District baseline: Hindu 74.05, Muslim 25.54 (A, Census 2011). AC 7 runs along Bangladesh border → Muslim share materially higher:

| Block | Hindu % | Muslim % | Other % |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dinhata-I | 62.73 | 36.98 | 0.29 |
| Dinhata-II | 63.11 | 36.68 | 0.21 |
| **AC 7 estimate (E)** | **~64–66** | **~34–36** | **~0.5** |

Bengali-Muslim (Sheikh / **Nasya-Sheikh** — Rajbanshi-descended Muslims demanding uplift, [Wikipedia Rajbanshi people](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rajbanshi_people)) dominant; no Urdu/Bihari-Muslim pocket.

**SC sub-composition** (E, Cooch Behar is state's Rajbanshi-core):
- **Rajbanshi (Hindu) ≈ 80–90% of SC pool** → **Hindu-Rajbanshi ~35–40% of AC population** (E, headline)
- Namasudra ~1–2% (minor)
- Other SC ~4–8%
- **UC bhadralok ~3–6%** (low; post-Partition East Bengali refugees small minority, concentrated Dinhata municipality)

**Ethnolinguistic Rajbanshis** include caste Rajbanshis who return "Bengali" on mother-tongue Census — true Rajbanshi identity share (caste+ethnicity) likely **35–45% of AC** (E).

## 4. Language

District: Bengali 94.79, Rajbongshi 1.31, Hindi 1.17 (A, Census 2011 C-16).

| Block | Bengali L1 % |
|---|---|
| Dinhata-I | 99.34 |
| Dinhata-II | 98.69 |

**AC Bengali ~99%** (A, block). But Rajbongshi/Kamtapuri given **official language status in WB in Feb 2018** ([Outlook PTI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rajbongshi_language)); ethnolinguistic Rajbanshi speakers massively under-counted in Census (most return "Bengali"). Self-identification rising but still census-minority.

## 5. Economic profile

- **Rural poverty** (NSS 55, 1999–2000): Cooch Behar district 25.62%; World Bank 2012 placed Cooch Behar in 20–26% BPL band (A/C)
- **Agri-labour heavy** (A, Census 2011 CDB):
  - Dinhata-I: cultivators 30.49%, ag-labourers 39.07%
  - **Dinhata-II: cultivators 33.51%, ag-labourers 51.06%** — extreme dependence, minimal non-farm
- Marginal land-holdings district-wide 77.6% (A/B, DSH 2013)
- Crop profile: Aman paddy, **jute** (very high — border belt), potatoes
- **Out-migration** moderate (below Matua/Murshidabad-Malda levels); Kerala (construction), Tamil Nadu (textiles)

## 9. Kamtapur / Greater Cooch Behar dynamics

- **Greater Cooch Behar People's Association** (Bansa Badan Barman) — argues 1949 merger conditional; UT/state demand
- **Kamtapur People's Party** (Atul Roy, 1996, Siliguri) — student wing **AKRSU**; **NDA ally** → KPP vote flows to BJP in 2026
- **Kamtapur Progressive Party** (2006 breakaway, merged back 2010)
- **KLO** militant outfit — dormant in Dinhata, symbolic reference
- **Net 2026 dynamic:** Rajbanshi identity vote (consolidated BJP 2019 → split 2021 bypoll → back to TMC 2024 LS). Basunia's 2024 LS win + BJP shifting Pramanik out of AC 7 → TMC favoured
